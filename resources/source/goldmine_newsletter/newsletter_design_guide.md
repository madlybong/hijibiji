# Goldmine Executive Market Intelligence - Newsletter Design Guide

## 1. Brand Identity & Overall Aesthetic
*   **Vibe:** Premium, Executive, Data-Dense but Clean, Financial.
*   **Target Audience:** High-net-worth individuals, institutional investors, and corporate executives.
*   **Core Principle:** Information density managed through strict grid alignments, card-based layouts, and clear typographic hierarchy.

## 2. Color Palette
The color system relies heavily on a dark/light contrast model, utilizing deep navy for impact and crisp white/off-white for readability.

*   **Primary Background (Covers & Accents):** Deep Navy Blue (`#061a33` approx). Conveys trust, authority, and depth.
*   **Secondary Background (Internal Pages):** Crisp White (`#ffffff`) or very faint off-white. Maximizes legibility for dense text and tables.
*   **Primary Text (Headers):** Deep Navy Blue (matching the cover).
*   **Body Text:** Dark Slate Gray (`#333333` approx). Softer than pure black for prolonged reading.
*   **Brand Accent:** Gold/Mustard Yellow (`#d4af37` approx). Used for the logo ("GOLDMINE"), thin divider lines, and primary callouts.
*   **Directional Indicators (Semantic Colors):**
    *   **Bullish/Positive:** Muted Green (e.g., for "BUY" ratings, positive YoY metrics).
    *   **Bearish/Negative:** Soft Red/Pink (e.g., for "Bearish Outlook", negative YoY metrics).
    *   *Note:* Avoid overly saturated neon greens or reds in tables; use pastel or muted tones for background fills (like in the Technical Analysis Bullish/Bearish case boxes).

## 3. Typography
The typographic hierarchy is essential for navigating the complex data.

*   **Font Family:** Clean, modern Sans-Serif (e.g., Montserrat, Inter, or Arial).
*   **Document Title / Headers:** All-caps, bold, often with wide letter-spacing (tracking). Example: `EXECUTIVE MARKET INTELLIGENCE`.
*   **Page Titles:** Massive, bold sans-serif (e.g., `MARKET OUTLOOK`). Usually accompanied by a smaller, colored sub-header above it (e.g., `NIFTY 50 — Technical Analysis` in gold).
*   **Data Points & Numbers:** Large, bold weight. Numbers must pop immediately.
*   **Body Copy:** Regular weight, comfortable line height (approx 1.5).

## 4. Layout & Grid System
The newsletter uses a highly structured, modular "card" and "dashboard" approach.

*   **Page Header:** Consistent across all internal pages.
    *   *Left:* Goldmine Logo (small).
    *   *Right:* "APRIL 2026 • EXECUTIVE MARKET INTELLIGENCE" (small, all-caps, tracked out).
    *   *Divider:* A thin gold/yellow line spanning the page width separates the header from the content.
*   **Page Footer:**
    *   *Left:* "Goldmine Stocks Pvt Ltd | research@goldmine.co.in" (gray, small).
    *   *Right:* Page number (large, bold).
*   **Dashboard Panels (Cards):**
    *   Information is chunked into logical blocks (cards) with very subtle gray borders or alternating light background colors.
    *   Use 3-column or 4-column grids for displaying multiple data points (like the Macro Dashboard or Commodity Dashboard).
*   **Tables:**
    *   *Headers:* Dark Navy Blue background with White text.
    *   *Rows:* Alternating subtle gray fills (zebra striping) for readability.
    *   *Borders:* Minimal vertical borders; rely on horizontal borders or row fills.

## 5. Graphical Elements & Data Visualization
*   **Technical Charts:** Candlestick charts are placed on stark black backgrounds to make the green/red candles and technical lines (RSI, MACD) pop. This mimics professional trading terminals.
*   **Line Charts (Macro Data):** Clean lines without aggressive gridlines. Use primary brand colors (Navy, Gold) for the lines.
*   **Directional Arrows:** Used universally in tables next to percentage changes (▲ green up, ▼ red down).
*   **Icons/Badges:** Numbered circles (e.g., 1, 2, 3, 4, 5) used for listing pillars or rationales to draw the eye sequentially.
*   **Visual Maps:** For technical price levels (Support/Resistance), use horizontal bar meters showing the current price relative to support and resistance zones.

## 6. Tone & Voice
*   **Editorial Style:** Objective, analytical, concise. Uses bullet points heavily rather than long paragraphs.
*   **Terminology:** Professional financial jargon (CAGR, EBITDA, TTM, Support/Resistance, Macro Headwinds) without excessive explanation, assuming an educated reader base.
